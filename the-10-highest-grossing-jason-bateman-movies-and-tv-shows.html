The 10 Highest-Grossing Jason Bateman Movies And TV Shows

Jason Bateman first came to prominence starring in the television sitcom Silver Spoons as Derek Taylor and then landed a recurring role in The Hogan Family. After acting in a string of unsuccessful television shows, he landed the lead role of Michael Bluth in the sitcom Arrested Development in 2003. The show became an instant hit for its fresh storyline, a stunning cast, and humor earning Bateman instant popularity worldwide. Since then, the actor has starred in a string of comedy movies that have made one of the most recognized comedy actors in Hollywood, known for his quirky yet intelligent characters on the screen.

From playing supporting characters that become one of the highlights of the movies to portraying leads that end up in a lot of trouble as the story progresses, every movie starring Bateman is an entertaining treat.

Couples Retreat (2009)

Couples Retreat is a romantic comedy and the directorial debut for Peter Billingsley starring Vince Vaughn, Jason Bateman, and Kristen Bell. Bateman portrayed the character of Jason Smith, the husband of Bell’s character Cynthia. Made on a $60 million budget, the movie was a box office success, earning $171.8 million worldwide, as stated by Cheat Sheet.

Dodgeball: A True Underdog Story (2004)

A sports comedy that sees Bateman pairing with Vince Vaughn and Ben Stiller, Dodgeball: A True Underdog Story is about a couple of misfits entering the dodgeball tournament in Las Vegas to save their local gym. Bateman played the supporting role of Pepper Brooks, and the movie earned $114.3 million at the box office.

Office Christmas Party (2016)

A Christmas comedy starring Jason Bateman, Jennifer Aniston, and Olivia Munn, Office Christmas Party was directed by Will Speck and Josh Gordon. It finished second at the box office during the opening weekend and grossed $114.5 million worldwide. The movie starring gifted comics with several cameos was well-received by audiences.

Horrible Bosses (2011)

Made on a $37 million budget, Seth Gordon directed Horrible Bosses. Bateman stars alongside Charlie Day and Jason Sudeikis in the R-rated comedy, a hilarious roller coaster from the beginning to the end credits. As mentioned by Rotten Tomatoes, the movie made $117.5 million at theaters around the world.

Game Night (2018)

A combination of action and comedy, Game Night sees a group of friends in a series of mysterious situations after a character is seemingly kidnapped. Bateman portrays the character of Max David, husband of Rachel McAdams’ character Annie. Against the budget of $37 million, Bateman’s movie managed to rake $117.7 million at the box office worldwide.

Identity Thief (2013)

A Seth Gordon-directed road comedy, Bateman stars as Sandy Patterson opposite a con artist played by Melissa McCarthy. The audiences were well-liked by the legendary comedic duo, and the movie opened at the top position at the box office, earning $39.5 million within the first weekend sales. It grossed a total of $174 million on a $35 million budget.

The Break-Up (2006)

A classic romantic comedy starring Jennifer Aniston and Vince Vaughn in the lead displays that love conquers all formulas seen in classic movies. The story follows the lead couple and their relationship during a break-up, as reported by The Guardian. Bateman has a small role in the movie, playing the couple’s friend and realtor Mark Riggleman. The movie earned $205.7 million on a $52 million budget, a box office success.

Juno (2007)

A coming-of-age comedy, Juno was written by and starred Elliot Page as the lead Juno MacGuff, a sixteen-year-old who gets pregnant. Page’s character decided to give the baby up for adoption, and Bateman portrays the character of Mark Loring, the prospective adoptive father, with Jennifer Garner as his wife. Made on a merely $7.5 million budget, the movie earned $231.4 million at the box office. Even if released in only seven theaters across America, the film surpassed all expectations.

Hancock (2008)

A superhero movie starring Will Smith in the titular role of John Hancock, an alcoholic superhero whose actions cause chaos in the city. Charlize Theron portrays the role of Mary Embrey, Hancock’s ex-wife, while Bateman plays her husband Ray. Made on a $150 million budget, the movie grossed $629.4 million in theaters around the world.

Zootopia (2016)

A computer-animated buddy cop film, Bateman lends his voice to the character of a small-time con artist Nick Wilde in Zootopia. According to Deadline, the movie became the second Disney movie of 2016 to enter the $1 billion club, making $1.024 billion. It became the fourth Disney animated movie to gross $1 billion after Frozen, Minions, and Toy Story 3.

Along with starring in comedies, Bateman has also worked in television shows. One of his recent drama shows is Ozark, a crime show that has earned several Emmys and Screen Actors Guild Awards. Bateman has earned $300,000 per episode for his portrayal and grossed over $30 million in net worth throughout his acting years. The actor was last seen starring in the Fourth Season, Part 1 of Ozark, in January 2022.
