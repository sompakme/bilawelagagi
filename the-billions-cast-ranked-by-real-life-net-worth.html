Highlights

  • Billions showcases power struggles in high finance, inspired by real financial crime.
  • Billions features groundbreaking representation, including the first non-binary character on American TV.
  • The star-studded cast of Billions comprises talented actors with impressive net worths.

Showtime's drama series Billions has been a benchmark in television storytelling for seven seasons, pulling back the curtain on the ruthless world of high finance.

Set against the backdrop of New York City and Connecticut, Billions follows the power struggles between two titans of finance: Bobby Axelrod (Damian Lewis), a hedge fund kingpin known for his aggressive tactics and relentless pursuit of wealth, and Chuck Rhoades (Paul Giamatti), a tenacious U.S. Attorney determined to bring Axe to justice.

The series draws inspiration from real-life financial crime prosecutions, offering a glimpse into the high-stakes world of Wall Street and government investigations.

Billions has also gained recognition for its groundbreaking inclusion.

The series holds the distinction of being the first American TV show to feature a non-binary character and actor, marking a significant step forward for representation on screen.

Naturally, a show focused on the world's financial elite also boasts a star-studded cast.

Heavy hitters like Paul Giamatti, Damian Lewis, Maggie Siff, and Corey Stoll bring proficiency to the complex narrative of Billions.

The show's popularity, alongside the actors' impressive resumes, raises a question: What are the net worths of the cast of Billions?

Let's find out.

6 Corey Stoll

Corey Stoll's Net Worth (Estimated) - $3 Million

Corey Stoll joined the cast of Billions for its fourth season, and remained a series regular until season six.

He portrayed Michael Prince, a hedge fund manager and entrepreneur who becomes a thorn in the side of Bobby Axelrod.

Stoll brought a steely intensity to the role, perfectly capturing Prince's ambition and willingness to challenge Axelrod's dominance. Stoll is a seasoned actor with over 15 years in the industry, boasting over 60 film credits.

While Ant-Man remains his biggest commercial success, grossing over $519 million worldwide, his filmography is filled with diverse roles. Stoll has established himself as a strong dramatic presence on both the big and small screen.

He is best known for his role as Congressman Peter Russo on House of Cards, a part that earned him a Golden Globe nomination.

Corey Stoll has an estimated net worth of $3 million.

5 David Costabile

David Costabile's Net Worth (Estimated) - $6 Million

In Billions, David Costabile delivers a charismatic performance as Mike "Wags" Wagner, Axe Capital's loyal COO (Chief Operating Officer).

Joining the series in 2016, Costabile has become synonymous with Wags' blend of sharp wit, unabashed hedonism, and fierce devotion to his boss, Bobby Axelrod.

His memorable one-liners and penchant for excess make Wags a beloved figure in the show's complex world. Costabile boasts a long and celebrated acting career spanning television, film, and theater.

His breakthrough roles include Detective Rick Messer in Damages, and Doug in Flight Of The Conchords. He also starred in the acclaimed series The Wire as Thomas Klebanow.

Notably, he has had significant roles in series such as Breaking Bad and Better Call Saul, as well as in films like The Dirt.

Costabile's cumulative film appearances have contributed significantly to his professional stature, complementing his extensive television work.

David Costabile currently has an estimated net worth of $6 million.

4 Maggie Siff

Maggie Siff's Net Worth (Estimated) - $8 Million

Maggie Siff brings a magnetic intensity to her portrayal of Wendy Rhoades, a psychiatrist, performance coach, and the wife of U.S. Attorney Chuck Rhoades.

Caught in the crossfire between her husband and his relentless pursuit of Bobby Axelrod, Wendy navigates a morally ambiguous landscape, her ambition and loyalties constantly tested.

Siff started her acting career in the early 2000s, and her breakthrough came with her role as Rachel Menken Katz on the iconic AMC drama Mad Men.

She followed this with success on the gritty FX series Sons Of Anarchy as Dr. Tara Knowles, a role that garnered her two Critics' Choice Television Award nominations.

On the big screen, Siff made her debut in the legal thriller Michael Clayton in 2007, and has since appeared in a variety of films.

Siff's filmography also includes roles in movies like Push and Funny People, both of which grossed $49 million and $71 million respectively.

Her skill in portraying morally conflicted characters and impressive versatility have contributed to her estimated net worth of $8 million, earned through several bodies of work.

3 Malin Akerman

Malin Akerman's Net Worth (Estimated) - $10 Million

Malin Akerman brought a touch of Hollywood glamour to Billions for its first season as Lara Ehrlich, wife of Axe Capital trader Charles Rhoades Jr.

While her character only appeared in a handful of episodes, Akerman imbued Lara with a sharp wit and a shrewd understanding of the power dynamics at play.

Akerman is a multi-talented entertainer with over 20 years in the industry. The Swedish actress has been around for over two decades.

Akerman has been a prominent figure in both the North American and international film industries. She began her career with roles in films like The Utopian Society and gained broader recognition with her performance in The Heartbreak Kid and 27 Dresses.

Her role as Silk Spectre II in Watchmen garnered her a Saturn Award nomination for "Best Supporting Actress."

Akerman has also featured in big-budget films like The Proposal and Couples Retreat, both of which achieved box office success, grossing over $317 million and $171 million respectively.

Akerman's consistent performances across various genres have helped her to accumulate an estimated net worth of $10 million.

2 Damian Lewis

Damian Lewis' Net Worth (Estimated) - $25 Million

Damian Lewis anchored Billions as the ruthless billionaire Bobby Axelrod for its first five seasons.

His performance exhibits a blend of raw intensity and subtle cunning, capturing the essence of a Wall Street titan whose maneuvers define the high stakes of finance and power.

From 2016 to 2021, Lewis appeared in 60 episodes, running the show with his layered acting. Lewis' career began in the 1990s, and he steadily built a reputation for taking on challenging roles.

His breakthrough came with his portrayal of U.S. Army Major Richard Winters in the acclaimed HBO miniseries Band Of Brothers.

Damian reinforced his reputation as an acting dynamo with his Golden Globe and Emmy-winning performance as Nicholas Brody in the Showtime series Homeland.

Lewis has also garnered critical acclaim for his historical portrayals, including Henry VIII in Wolf Hall.

Lewis has appeared in several films, including Our Kind Of Traitor, and a role alongside Brad Pitt in Quentin Tarantino's Once Upon A Time In Hollywood.

Though his filmography may be less expansive than some of his peers, Lewis demonstrates a discerning eye for projects that spotlight his remarkable range and dramatic depth.

Damian Lewis has an estimated net worth of $25 million.

1 Paul Giamatti

Paul Giamatti's Net Worth (Estimated) - $25 Million

A veteran character actor with over 30 years in the industry, Paul Giamatti brought his gravitas to Billions for seven seasons.

He played Chuck Rhoades Jr., the U.S. Attorney, in a constant power struggle with hedge fund king Bobby Axelrod (Damian Lewis).

Reports suggest Giamatti's earnings per episode on Billions reached $250,000 per episode. Giamatti's career spans over three decades, with a filmography highlighting his extensive flexibility.

Giamatti has starred in over 80 films, with a combined box office gross exceeding $6.5 billion. After years of perfecting his art in more minor roles, his breakout came in Sideways, earning him an Academy Award nomination.

He has since starred in critically acclaimed films like Cinderella Man and The Holdovers, the latter exceeding expectations with a box office gross of over $42 million.

In 2014, Giamatti played the villain "The Rhino" in The Amazing Spider-Man 2, his highest-grossing film, with a global total of $708 million.

Television audiences know Giamatti from his Emmy-winning performance as John Adams in the HBO miniseries John Adams and his role in Downton Abbey.

Giamatti appeared in the action-packed thriller Gunpowder Milkshake, and his supporting role in Jungle Cruise reportedly earned him a $300,000 paycheck.

Paul Giamatti has an estimated net worth of $25 million.
