The Most Expensive Restaurants In Miami, As Of 2023

Miami is known for its vibrant nightlife, beautiful beaches, and delicious cuisine. With a plethora of restaurants to choose from, it's not surprising that some of them can cost a pretty penny. In this article, you will discover the 8 most expensive restaurants in Miami as of 2023. From luxurious waterfront locations to celebrity chef-owned establishments, these restaurants offer a dining experience like no other. Whether you're a local looking for a special occasion spot or a visitor looking to indulge, these restaurants are sure to leave a lasting impression. So, let's take a closer look at the extravagant world of high-end dining in Miami.

Nobu Miami - $95

Nobu Miami is one of the top spots for all first-time visitors to the city. Once, it was a coastal club area that was extremely stylish. Yet, its proximity to the seaside today makes it an ideal dining destination for tourists. Surprisingly, it is a Japanese restaurant with the renowned chef Nobu Matsuhisa. Hence, customers will certainly get the best dining experience here. The traditional lanterns, cherry blossom decorations, and authentic Japanese cuisine have made it one of the finest. Being one of the most expensive restaurants in Miami, its menu features all the delectable delicacies that will linger in the diner's memory for years.

Bourbon Steak by Michael Mina - $118

This restaurant's stunning ambiance will leave you speechless. The American restaurant Bourbon Steak by Michael Mina is renowned as one of the most expensive steakhouses. In Miami, you will not find traditional steak recipes like these anyplace else. Hormone-free meat, lamb, and hog, as well as all organic foods, are served. In addition to distinctive wines and fresh, locally sourced shellfish, diners can enjoy a variety of other delicacies. There are handcrafted cocktails available for all guests. You can make an online reservation and prepare to enjoy the culinary culture of Miami. The award-winning chef Mina inspired and developed the table settings.

Pao by Paul Qui - $120

Among the most expensive restaurants in Miami, this one has the most extravagant decor. You will not encounter the motif as it exists in Miami. Dazzling gold and crimson hues permeate the unicorn-themed environment. Pao by Paul Qui is an Asian restaurant with a distinctive specialty drink. You must give this a try. Plus, they serve it in a copper unicorn cup, which is an elegant touch. Nevertheless, in order to drink its famous cocktail, you must pay $25. Moreover, the Wagyu Carpaccio is a must-try dish at Pao.

Palme d'Or - $186

This upscale French restaurant's stunning ambiance will leave you in awe. It is constructed within the lobby of The Biltmore Hotel and is encircled by the hotel's historic swimming pool. The dining set is inspired by the fashion of the 1920s. It will transport customers to a golden age of luxury where they can sample French cuisine. Chef Gregory Pugin, the nomination for emerging star chef, and James Beard rule the kitchen. Guests can enjoy the Pugin-designed ni-course menu. Zuma - $200

This Japanese restaurant offers diners a unique experience. In contrast to traditional Japanese food, its greatest restaurants have a casual dining and drinking culture, traditional yet modern Japanese cuisine, and a fantastic atmosphere. Diners can find something new and delectable at Zuma. You can enjoy your evening with food from the restaurant's three kitchens, including the robata grill, sushi counter, and central kitchen. Surprisingly, private eating is available in its two distinct rooms. The beachfront patio is without a doubt an ideal spot for intimate meals.

Hakkasan - $208

In addition to Japanese and Asian cuisine, Chinese cuisine is also available in Miami. Since its debut in 2009, this has been a momentous occasion in Miami. Hakkasan in Miami has set the bar for quality and standard. Due to its exceptional Cantonese cuisine, it continues to raise the bar for restaurants and is popular among both locals and tourists. It offers a luxurious atmosphere, excellent wine, and fresh food to make your dining experience memorable. You may select one of the restaurant's sample trademark meals and pair it with a light wine. About the interior design, the dining set will astound you. Although it can drain your bank account, the experience will last a lifetime.

La Sirenuse Miami - $210

As the setting of "La Sirenuse Miami," the Amalfi coast of Italy is explored. This hotel features a beautiful and sophisticated style of decor. Hence, it is the most luxurious restaurant in the city that serves some of the finest Italian dishes. It has established a unique and standard position among Miami's most expensive restaurants. You can start the evening with champagne at Le Sirenuse Miami and dine with loved ones while taking in the scenery of the ocean. The meals on the menu are inspired by the traditions of the Amalfi Coast. Chef Antonio Mermolia brings seasonal flavors to the table.

NAOE - $280

This five-star restaurant in Miami offers a unique dining experience. Each night, Chef Kevin Cory prepares a unique dinner for his guests. So, every night there is the best offer in Naoe for a three-hour dining experience. So, allow three to four days for a gourmet eating experience. There are two separate time periods available for reservations: 6:30 PM or 9:30 PM. It is an eight-seat restaurant with a fixed-price menu that only includes Chef Kevin Cory. Obviously, it is pricey since it is five-star but it is worthwhile to visit. Visitors from Miami and all over the world come to this restaurant to taste the chef-style cuisine.
