Who is Tiger Woods?

A professional golfer, Tiger Woods won the US Masters at Augusta National in 1997, at 21, with a record score, making him the youngest man and the first African American to have multiple golf records and various accolades. Over the next 12 years, Woods won another 13 majors and was voted PGA Player of the Year ten times.

In October 2019, Woods competed in his first 2020 PGA Tour tournament, the Zozo Championship, Japan’s first-ever PGA Tour event. The news of his multiple leg injuries in a single-car accident in Los Angeles in February 2021 was reported. Biography TimeLine

Tiger Woods is a retired professional golfer and one of the most successful athletes ever. Born on December 30, 1975, in Cypress, California, Woods showed an early talent for golf and quickly rose through the ranks of the amateur and professional golf circuits. He is known for his dominance of the sport in the late 1990s and early 2000s, during which he won numerous major championships and set several records. Despite facing a series of personal and professional setbacks, Woods remains one of his generation’s most iconic and influential athletes.

  • December 30, 1975: Eldrick Tont Woods (later known as Tiger Woods) was born in Cypress, California.
  • 1984: Woods wins his first golf tournament at the age of eight.
  • 1991: He becomes the youngest player to win the US Junior Amateur Championship at 15.
  • 1992: Woods wins his second US Junior Amateur Championship.
  • 1994: He becomes the youngest player to win the US Amateur Championship at 18.
  • 1996: Woods turns professional and signs endorsement deals with Nike and Titleist.
  • 1997: He wins his first major championship, the Master’s Tournament, with a record-breaking 18 under par.
  • 1999-2002: Woods dominates professional golf, winning eight major championships and simultaneously completing the “Tiger Slam” by holding all four major titles.
  • 2009: Woods’ personal life becomes the subject of intense media scrutiny following revelations of extramarital affairs.
  • 2010-2013: Woods struggles with injuries and poor form but still wins several tournaments.
  • 2017: He undergoes back surgery and takes an extended break from professional golf.
  • 2019: Woods wins his fifth Masters Tournament, his first major championship in over a decade.
  • February 23, 2021: Woods is involved in a severe car accident in California, suffering multiple leg injuries.

Childhood and Early Life

Born as Eldrick Tont Woods in Cypress, California, on December 30, 1975, to an African American Army officer, father, and a Thai mother, Woods learned to play golf as a child. He has two half-brothers and a half-sister from his father’s first marriage.   

Woods’ father began calling him “Tiger” as a young boy, honouring a fellow soldier and buddy with the same nickname. He was a student at Western High School in Anaheim.

Relationship Status

About Woods’s relationship status, Erica Herman, a restaurant manager, has been his girlfriend since late 2017. In 2004, he married his ex-wife, Elin Nordegren, a former Swedish model. They were the parents of two children, a daughter named Sam Alexis Woods and Charlie Axel Woods.

Reports of his extramarital encounters with many women began to surface in 2009. Even though he initially denied it, he finally admitted to his wrongdoings with his wife. In 2010, Woods and his wife divorced as a result of this.

Professional Career

Before becoming a professional in 1996, Woods attended Stanford University and won a series of amateur golf events in the United States. He was instantly given partnerships with Nike, Inc., and Titleist after becoming a professional golfer in 1996, owing to his undergraduate success.

He won his first major tournament, the Masters, in 1997, and again in the Official World Golf Rankings; he was rated No. 1. In 1999, he won the PGA Championship. The US Open was one of his triumphs in 2000, as he broke or tied nine records. Tiger won multiple golf trophies during that season, including the 2001 Masters Tournament. Tiger became the first player to win all four major professional titles simultaneously.

However, in late 2008, he was forced to pause due to knee surgery. In 2009, Tiger was warmly welcomed back to the game after recovery. He did not disappoint his supporters and delivered an outstanding performance to win the 2009 Presidents Cup. By 2013, he had returned to his former self. In January, he won the Farmers Insurance Open, and in March, he won the WGC-Cadillac Championship. He was re-established as the World’s No. 1 golfer with a victory at the Arnold Palmer Invitational.

Woods had his sixth microdiscectomy surgery on his back on December 23, 2020; the update was that he had a successful surgery. In early January 2021, the Netflix documentary Tiger was released on his professional life.
