WWE RAW Viewership Dips On 2/12, Show Ranks First On Cable

WWE RAW viewership dipped on Monday night.

Wrestlenomicsreports that, per PWTorch and Programming Insider, RAW averaged 1.747 million viewers on Monday. This number is down from last week, when RAW averaged 1.889 million viewers.

In the 18 to 49 demographic, RAW scored a 0.56 rating. This is also down from last week (0.58 rating). RAW ranked #1 on cable on Monday and was only outranked on broadcast by The Bachelor on ABC (0.61 rating).

The February 12 episode of WWE RAW featured the fallout of the WrestleMania 40 Kickoff press conference. Cody Rhodes sent a message to The Rock, and Seth Rollins told him he did not have to fight alone. In the main event, Sami Zayn faced Shinsuke Nakamura. The show also featured three Elimination Chamber qualifying matches.
